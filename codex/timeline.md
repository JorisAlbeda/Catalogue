# Timeline of the Oathsworn Empires

## Year 0 of the Old Oak: The Slum Deal

In the back alleys of a slum, two men conspired to overthrow the Clockwork Prince and founded the First Empire in the mountains.
**Editor's comments:** This name has been criticised by many: surely the First Empire would have been the Clockwork Prince's regime?
Not only that, but the Year 0 of the Old Oak implies that the Old Oak first hatched at that time, while it has been known for years that the Old Oak predates this year by multiple centuries. Still, these are the terms they have been given, and we historians have to live with that.

## Year 30 of the Old Oak: Clockwork Rebellion

The Clockwork Prince, exiled and humbled, returned in an attempt to incite rebellion in the First Empire. He was defeated once again, however, and left the lands for good.

## Year 50 of the Old Oak: Oathbreaker

The First Empire was challenged by several outlandish warlords, one of whom broke their eternal pact to side with the Empire. His family line is still known as the Oathbreaker family, and it is said the curse his former allies placed on him still runs through his blood.
The war ended in a rebellion from within, leaving a successor to take the throne. The regime under this successor's rule later became known as the Broken Empire.

## Year 150 of the Old Oak: Battle of the Valleys

Chancellor Aldric Vane, the first Chancellor of whom we have the recorded name, found his rule challenged by Selene Claire and Iven Marrow. Despite being aided by Ser Corvin Thale, he suffered defeat at the hands of Selene Claire, who founded the Supreme Empire in the Fertile Valley.

## Year 243 of the Old Oak: The Sorcerer of the Woods

Chancellor Halvard Cray defended the Fertile Valley, the Charming Valley, and the woods against Wren Sable and the Sorcerer of the Woods. He was allied with Isolde Varn, who fell in battle against the Sorcerer and his Fire Talkers. The Sorcerer eventually founded a new Empire around the Old Oak, and introduced the year counting system we use today. Together with Wren Sable, he made a pact for a kinder yet more truthful world: The Sorcerer swore the Oath of Protection, and Wren Sable founded the Order of Truthwatchers.
Thus began the Woodland Empire.

## Year 309 of the Old Oak: A sudden departure

On the Sorcerer's daughter Lilianne's 18th birthday, he vanished from the realms, leaving her as the youngest Chancellor the Old Oak has known. Before long, petty thefts and acts of defiance began, challenging the young ruler on her rule.

## Year 310 of the Old Oak: The Bandit Queen

Chancellor Lilianne proved herself against Corvin Varn, Perrin Ashe and Sorrel Wick, whom she exiled after uncovering her planned coup against the Woodland Empire. In the struggle, she used the Firetalker's power of the Verdflayme once more, burning many enemies alive. Additionally, she found the Bandit Crown, making this the first time in history that bandits allied themselves with the Empire, bowing before her as the Bandit Queen.
The war ended with a truce, in which Perrin Ashe and Corvin Varn founded the country of Ashvale. Lilianne swore never to bury the flame that had killed so many, to break the cycle.

## Year 410 of the Old Oak: The Darkest Flame

The current year. The buried fire has become a thing of legends, and is now known known as The Darkest Flame. Whispers speak of its power. It is said that the palace in the Deep Woods still holds the Darkest Secret, a tome that tells of how to conjure the Darkest Flame itself. There are those in the lands bordering the Woodland Empire, not the least in Ashvale, who believe the Flame is a symbol for humanity's spark, and does not belong buried under the ground. Others simply seek to claim its power for themselves.
It seems war is once again brewing in the Woodland Empire....
