# Trade at Tinker's Fair

## Description
Trade at Tinker's Fair involved exchanging secrets and favors to gain allies and resources. Sorrel traded two secrets for favor and gathered fresh hands at the fair before deciding to keep her strength close. Corvin considered trading his remaining resources but ultimately chose to return to a way station. The event allowed participants to secure support through strategic exchanges, such as trading a secret for a favor toward a banner. It became a critical moment for navigating conflicts in the region, with outcomes affecting alliances and movements. The fair served as a hub for these transactions, influencing decisions about warfare and diplomacy.

## History
The practice of trading at Tinker's Fair emerged as a strategic necessity during conflicts over Charming Valley and the Hinterland. Sorrel used the fair to secure resources by trading secrets for favors, while Corvin faced decisions about whether to engage in trade or retreat. Hesper participated by leveraging her reputation to raise funds, and Lilianne later confronted Sorrel over her actions. The fair became a recurring site for such exchanges, with participants like Fen Quill and Marlow Ash involved in transactions that shaped the game's dynamics. These trades often had lasting consequences, such as Sorrel reclaiming a banner or Corvin's inability to challenge the mine directly. The event's significance grew as it influenced broader conflicts, including interactions with the Ruined Temple and Narrow Pass.
