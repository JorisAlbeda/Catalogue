# The Oath

## Description
The Oath was a binding vow sworn by leaders to uphold justice and protect their people, though its demands often forced them to confront moral dilemmas. It became a symbol of leadership, tied to the role of Chancellor, yet its weight could drive even the strongest to question their choices. Lilianne of the Woods struggled with its meaning, torn between her father's legacy and her own resolve.

## History
The Oath originated as a pledge taken by Chancellors to safeguard their realms, but its true test lay in the willingness to make painful sacrifices. Lilianne's father abandoned his former methods, choosing peace over violence, which marked the difference between a ruler who upheld the Oath and one who did not. Sorrel later claimed the throne by proving her commitment to the Oath's principles, ensuring its legacy endured.
