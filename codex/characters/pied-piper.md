# Pied Piper

## Description
Pied Piper was a figure recruited during a Search in the Cradle discard pile at Charming Valley but was later retracted. It was initially mistaken for a Beast-suited card due to its Order-adjacent appearance, despite not being a Beast. The Ruined Temple's standing effect barred anyone but Perrin from playing Beast cards, which led to its rejection. It remained unclaimed, neither belonging to the Empire nor to Perrin. The piper was a symbol of ambiguity, tied to neither side, and left without a clear allegiance.

## History
Pied Piper was drawn during a Search in the Cradle discard pile at Charming Valley, where it was briefly recruited but then retracted. The Ruined Temple's standing effect, which restricted Beast card play to Perrin alone, caused its rejection despite its Order-adjacent appearance. The piper was never officially claimed by any faction, remaining a loose entity. It was considered a potential ally but was ultimately left unclaimed, as no one sought to control it. The narrative suggests it was a figure of intrigue, tied to the Ruined Temple's rules and the broader conflicts of the time. Its status as unclaimed reflected the tensions between factions and the limitations imposed by the game's mechanics.
