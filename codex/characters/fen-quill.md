# Fen Quill

## Description
Fen Quill was a light-fingered individual known for testing what people did not want taken, often using his ability to extract secrets from others. He was described as a man who checked the shape of a room through small rituals, such as turning over clay jars without intent. Fen Quill was associated with subtle actions, like testing inkwells for loose coins before conversation began. His presence was marked by a quiet, observational nature, often lingering near shelves or unattended objects. He was part of Sorrel's inner circle, aiding her in securing favors and information. His actions were deliberate, balancing risk and reward in his interactions.

## History
Fen Quill was first linked to Sorrel when she recruited him to aid in making Charming Valley harder to deceive. He played a role in her strategies, such as trading secrets for favors and gathering intelligence. He was brought into the open at Tinker's Fair, where he facilitated her acquisition of a favor. Fen Quill's actions included slipping into the Chancellor's hall to deliver messages, demonstrating his connection to political intrigue. He was involved in Sorrel's efforts to rebuild her influence, including securing the Buried Giant as a site under her control. His interactions with Sorrel and others were marked by a blend of utility and caution, reflecting his role as both an informant and strategist.
