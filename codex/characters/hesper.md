# Hesper

## Description
Hesper was the keeper of an old way station on the Hinterland road, long before she became part of Corvin's circle. She revealed her full face to Corvin at Tinker's Fair, the evening before a trade, after years of keeping her appearance hidden. She could drain entire treasuries of coins and send them wherever wandering folk stored their own, but each act cost her a secret that vanished entirely. Her actions always came with a visible flinch, as if the price of her power weighed on her. She was a figure of quiet resilience, tied to the land and its shifting alliances. Her presence was both a burden and a necessity in Corvin's dealings.

## History
Hesper was raised and lost in the same visit that cost Sorrel her place among the Chancellor's followers. She never mourned what was lost, as nothing remained worth the treasure Corvin still carried. Corvin used her name at Tinker's Fair to raise coin for a banner taken from Sorrel, reclaiming it the same afternoon. She guided Corvin away from the mine's garrison, preventing a futile fight. Her ability to manipulate coin and secrets made her indispensable, even as she bore the cost of her actions. She stood as a bridge between Corvin's past and the shifting loyalties of the Hinterland.
