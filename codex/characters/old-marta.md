# Old Marta

## Description
Old Marta was a wandering seer known for her cryptic prophecies and enigmatic presence. She was described as sitting motionless near a door, as if awaiting someone to collect a debt in blood. Her gaze was fixed forward, never raising her voice yet seeming perpetually alert. She wore a plain dark coat, her appearance marked by a quiet intensity. Her words carried weight, shaping the fates of those who listened. Her prophecies were both a burden and a guide, forcing others to confront truths they might have preferred to ignore.

## History
Old Marta first encountered Corvin at the Mine, where she foretold a future he was unsure he wanted to hear. Her prophecy about his rise to the People's Favor was read aloud by Corvin himself, leaving it open for others to witness. She remained at a way station, her words influencing Corvin's choices as he gathered allies. Her vision of his future was a banner he held three times before the war ended, though the promised morning never arrived. She guided Corvin to discard false paths, keeping only the soldier who vowed a victory would pay for itself. Her words became part of the Oath's condition, shaping the course of events even after she was no longer present.
