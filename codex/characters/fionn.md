# Fionn

## Description
Fionn was a figure known for his ability to persuade caravans to take indirect routes through friendly territory, often redirecting them toward Perrin's roads. He was a quiet advisor to Perrin, staying close at Fertile Valley throughout the war. Fionn counted wagon wheels under his breath before departures, claiming it was for luck. His presence was subtle, more a whispered suggestion than an active participant in events. He was already at Fertile Valley's side at the war's start, remaining there until the end.

## History
Fionn's influence began at the war's onset, where he was already aligned with Perrin at Fertile Valley. He operated as a strategist, guiding caravans and shaping movements without direct confrontation. His methods relied on persuasion and subtle manipulation, ensuring loyalty through unspoken debts. Despite his importance, he remained an enigmatic figure, never fully committing to any cause beyond his own interests. His role diminished as Perrin's authority grew, but his impact lingered in the shifting paths of those he influenced.

Deceased
