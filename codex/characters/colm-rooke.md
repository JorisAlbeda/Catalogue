# Colm Rooke

## Description
Sergeant Colm Rooke was a soldier who swore that any real victory Corvin won would pay for itself. He was known for his blunt honesty, answering questions with further questions, and his unwavering loyalty to Corvin. He earned rewards from the old military treasury for every fight he won outright, yet kept a list of commendations he was never officially given. Before battles, he would recite this list as if it were a sacred ritual. Colm was also a trusted messenger, delivering warnings and questions to Perrin across the war-torn lands.

## History
Colm was sent ahead by Corvin multiple times to warn Perrin or ask him critical questions about the war's outcome. He first met Perrin at the edge of the Hinterland camp, where he delivered a warning about Sorrel Wick's impending victory. Later, he was tasked with asking Perrin about an old key tied to the war's resolution. Colm's messages often involved high-stakes decisions, such as the fate of the Narrow Pass and the final confrontation at the Mine. His final mission was to question Perrin about the key, knowing there might be no time left for answers. Colm's actions were pivotal in shaping the war's final moments.
