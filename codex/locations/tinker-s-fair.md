# Tinker's Fair

## Description

Tinker's Fair was a transient gathering place where traders and wanderers converged, known for its bustling exchange of goods and information. It served as a hub for those seeking to barter secrets, recruit allies, and secure resources. The fair was frequented by individuals like Sorrel and Hesper, who used it to negotiate deals and amass influence. It was a neutral ground where temporary alliances were formed and strategic plans were discussed.

## History

Tinker's Fair emerged as a critical meeting point during periods of conflict and negotiation, particularly in the Cradle. It was a place where leaders like Sorrel and Corvin gathered resources and manpower, trading favors and secrets to strengthen their positions. The fair's existence was intertwined with the region's history of shifting power dynamics, as it allowed for temporary coalitions and the exchange of critical information. It was a site of both commerce and subterfuge, where deals were struck and betrayals were concealed. The fair's role diminished as conflicts evolved, but its legacy remained in the strategies of those who once relied on its networks. Its eventual decline was marked by the dispersal of its participants and the fading of its influence in the region.
