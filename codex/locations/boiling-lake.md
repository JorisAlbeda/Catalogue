# Boiling Lake

## Description
Boiling Lake was a place of constant turmoil, its waters perpetually churning with steam and heat. A pot was always kept boiling there, a task maintained by a figure who ensured the fire never died, even during sleep. Anyone who wandered onto the lake's surface without permission met a grim fate, often losing their life in blood or leaving behind two men as payment. The lake itself was considered an angry entity, its simmering contents a sign of its restless nature. The area around it was marked by the presence of bandits, who had once claimed it as their own. Despite the dangers, the lake remained a focal point of power and conflict in the region.

## History
Boiling Lake was first claimed by Corvin Varn during the war's opening days, when he wrested control of Fertile Valley and the lake from bandits. The battle cost him two men, but he secured the area and its resources. However, his rule was short-lived, as he lost both Fertile Valley and the lake to Perrin Ashe within the same week. The lake's existence was tied to the bandits who had previously inhabited the region, and its volatile nature made it a contested site. Corvin's acquisition of the lake was part of his broader campaign to establish dominance, though its dangers ensured it remained a place of both power and peril. The lake's connection to the bandits and its ever-boiling waters made it a symbol of the region's turbulent history.
