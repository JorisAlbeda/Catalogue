# Ruined hall

## Description
The Ruined hall was once a grand structure, its original purpose obscured by time. It stood as a testament to the empire's former power, with remnants of its stonework and shattered archways hinting at its former grandeur. Moss and ivy crept over weathered carvings, while broken columns leaned precariously, their bases cracked by centuries of neglect. The hall's central chamber, once a throne room, had collapsed partially, leaving a gaping void where the central pillar had fallen. Its walls bore faded inscriptions, though their meaning had been lost to history. The site became a silent witness to the shifting tides of power, its ruins marking the end of an era.

## History
The Ruined hall was the seat of power for Perrin's grandfather's empire, a place where decrees were issued and alliances forged. After the empire's decline, the structure fell into disrepair, its halls echoing with the absence of life. It became a meeting ground for exiles and rebels, including Perrin Ashe and Corvin, who gathered there to plot the future of their fractured lands. The hall's remnants were later associated with the truce that led to the formation of Ashvale, a new order born from the ashes of the old regime. Its ruins also held symbolic weight, as the Horned Mask—a relic of the empire—was last offered there before being rejected. The site remained a haunting reminder of the past, its stones cold and unyielding beneath the feet of those who sought to reshape the world.
