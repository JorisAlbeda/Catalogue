# The village

## Description
The village was an empty stretch of ground situated beside a mine in the Hinterland. It was a place where Corvin Varn sought answers about a cauldron guarded by the mine. The villagers, who were wary of outsiders, provided him with information in exchange for his passage. The village lacked permanent structures, relying on temporary shelters, and its people were known for their cryptic warnings. Corvin left the village with a keeper of an old way station, rather than the mine's own workers, as the villagers' knowledge proved more valuable. The village's existence was tied to the mine's secrets, though its population had dwindled to a few individuals.

## History
The village emerged as a settlement near the mine in the Hinterland during the war's early days. Corvin Varn encountered it while searching for the mine's cauldron, which he could not afford to claim. The villagers, distrustful of strangers, communicated with him through messengers and offered guidance in exchange for safe passage. Their interactions led Corvin to abandon the mine's claims in favor of securing a way station keeper, who would grant him free passage later. The village's decline was marked by its abandonment, leaving only a few inhabitants who faded into obscurity. Its legacy is preserved only in the accounts of those who passed through, including Corvin, who noted its emptiness and the villagers' enigmatic warnings.
