# The mill

## Description
The mill stood at the edge of Fertile Valley, its stone walls weathered by time and conflict. It served as a neutral ground where Perrin and Corvin met to discuss their shared struggles. Inside, a key hung on a nail, its purpose shrouded in mystery until the war's end. Perrin later cleared the sluice, making the mill functional again after years of neglect. The structure bore witness to their uneasy truce, with Bettina Marsh often watching from the door. Its wheel turned more easily in the spring, a sign of the peace that followed.

## History
The mill was a contested site during the war, held by Corvin before Perrin claimed it. Perrin used it as a base to rally forces and declare his ambition to reclaim lost lands. Corvin visited the mill twice, once to negotiate and again to settle debts, though neither man fully understood the key's significance. After the war, the mill became a symbol of reconciliation, with Perrin ensuring its operations resumed. The first harvest in Ashvale was celebrated there, marking the end of conflict over the land. By the war's end, the mill stood as a testament to the uneasy peace between former rivals.
