# Signet ring

## Description
The Signet ring was a family heirloom, passed down through generations before it was sold off during a difficult year. It was resized twice by Corvin's grandmother, as jeweler's hesitated to ask why a dead woman's ring kept needing to fit new hands. The ring became associated with debts and unresolved obligations, carrying a legacy of unspoken grievances. Its absence was linked to a habit of not declaring debts settled, even when they seemed resolved. The ring's presence was tied to a sense of duty, as those who carried fire in their blood inherited both its weight and the burden of its history. It was a symbol of unfulfilled promises and a reminder of the family's fractured past.

## History
The Signet ring left the family within a generation, sold during a lean year that no one wished to name. It was replaced by a scrap of grey-green cloth and a habit of silence regarding debts. Corvin inherited the ring from his grandmother, who resized it twice to accommodate new wearers. The ring became a focal point of unresolved tensions, with some in Ashvale calling its absence heresy. It was tied to a debt owed to a clan, buried rather than settled, and passed down as a reminder of unclaimed inheritance. Those who carried fire in their blood inherited both the ring and the responsibility to decide whether reclaiming it meant digging it up or choosing another path. Its legacy endured through whispered stories and unspoken obligations.
