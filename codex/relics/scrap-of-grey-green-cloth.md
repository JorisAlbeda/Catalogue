# Scrap of grey-green cloth

## Description
The Scrap of grey-green cloth was a small, unwashed fragment of fabric, inherited without explanation and carried without question by its bearer. It was pinned inside a coat, its edges worn soft from years of being handled but never examined. The cloth bore no markings, yet it held weight in the hands of those who carried it, as if it carried the memory of a name never spoken. It was passed from one person to another, its origin and purpose left unacknowledged, yet its presence felt like an unspoken debt. The fabric seemed to fade into the folds of its owner's clothing, as though it had always belonged there. Its color, a muted blend of grey and green, mirrored the earth itself, as if it had been plucked from the ground rather than woven.

## History
The cloth was carried by Sorrel Wick, who inherited it without knowing its significance, though she treated it with quiet reverence. It was tied to a woman named Isolde, whose name was never spoken aloud, yet the cloth seemed to carry her memory. Sorrel passed it to Corvin Varn before a decisive moment, folding it with care and attaching it to a ring as if sealing a promise. The cloth’s history was tied to a debt left unresolved, a question left unanswered, and a legacy that outlived its original owner. It was mentioned in a letter from Chancellor Lilianne, who referenced it as part of a larger mystery, though its true purpose remained unclear. The cloth’s journey ended in Charming Valley, where it was catalogued as part of a surviving habit rather than a displayed artifact, its story left to echo in the silence of its keeper’s hands.
