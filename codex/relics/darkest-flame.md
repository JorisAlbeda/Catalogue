# The Darkest Flame

## Description

The Darkest Flame, Sticky Fire, Verdflayme. It was a flame contained in a glass orb, a weapon granted to Lilianne by the Old Oak. It was designed to end conflicts by burning all remnants of disagreement, leaving nothing to fuel further strife. The relic's power required a secret to be whispered to the orb, costing its wielder dearly. It was carried by those who sought to control or end battles, often with dire consequences. Its use was tied to the balance of power, as seen in its role during the war and its eventual burial. The orb's presence symbolized both destruction and the weight of choices made in its name.

## History

The Darkest Flame was granted by the Old Oak to the First Fire Talker under the name Verdflayme, though its exact origin remains obscured. It became a weapon of influence, used to sway outcomes in conflicts by burning through opposing forces.

During the Sorcerer's War, the Sorcerer of the Woods convinced the Fire Talkers to break their bonds with Halvard Cray's Empire, and they then fought at his side, notably at the Battle for the Drowned City, where they defeated Isolde Varn's armies with Verflayme, leading to her death.

Sixty years later, the Old Oak granted the Sticky Fire to Lilianne at the start of the war. She used it to slaughter Sorrel Wick's forces at the Firestorm at Buried Giant. After the war, Lilianne chose to bury Sticky Fire, ending its cycle of destruction. The relic's final resting place became a memorial, symbolizing peace over violence. Its legacy endured in stories and superstitions, even as its physical form was lost, and it became known as the Darkest Flame.

It is said that the palace in the Deep Woods still holds the Darkest Secret, a tome that tells of how to conjure the Darkest Flame itself...
