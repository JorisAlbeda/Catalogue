# Halvard Cray's old iron key

## Description
Halvard Cray's old iron key was a simple, rusted tool hung on a nail at the mill in Fertile Valley. It was said to open a strongbox hidden beneath the millstone, a vault untouched for generations. The key's purpose was tied to a letter Halvard Cray had written, meant for whoever might one day seek the truth about his legacy. It was never used to claim land or power, but instead to reveal a personal confession about the valley's true value. The key remained on the nail, checked regularly by Bettina Marsh, who guarded it for decades. Its presence symbolized unspoken debts and unresolved conflicts from Halvard's reign.

## History
Halvard Cray crafted the key during his reign as a symbol of his ambition to reclaim lost territories. It was part of a vault he sealed, containing a letter addressed to future seekers of truth. The key was entrusted to Bettina Marsh, who kept it on the nail at the mill since before Perrin Ashe was born. During the war, Perrin discovered the key's existence and used it to open the vault, revealing Halvard's letter. The key's discovery became a turning point, exposing Halvard's mistake in valuing land over principles. Its legacy endured as a reminder of the war's unresolved questions and the weight of inherited struggles.
