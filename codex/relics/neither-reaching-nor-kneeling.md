# Neither Reaching Nor Kneeling

## Description

Neither Reaching Nor Kneeling was a painting depicting a woman in a plain dark coat facing a drawn blade, her hands open and empty. The ledger accompanying it recorded her name as Wick, noting her exile twice, disappearance once, and no formal record of her death. It was associated with a legend that bandits at Narrow Pass grew polite due to her influence.

## History

The relic originated from a ledger and painting linked to a woman exiled twice and vanished once, with no official record of her death. It became tied to folklore about calming bandits at Narrow Pass, though no living person could confirm or deny the tale. The painting's title, Neither Reaching Nor Kneeling, reflected her refusal to submit or conquer.
