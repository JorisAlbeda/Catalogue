# Converse — Lilianne & Naiad (Round 9, #2 — post-game)

**Setting:** Lilianne's war tent atop Buried Giant, the night after the
decisive battle that ended the war (Sorrel's forces destroyed by Sticky
Fire, Perrin surrendered, Lilianne confirmed Oathkeeper as War
Exhaustion ends the game). Naiad finds her alone, hollow-eyed, after
she signs Sorrel's banishment to Narrow Pass.

**Lilianne:** "I don't like this, mother."

**Naiad:** *(kneeling to her level)* "Say the rest of it. 'I don't like
this' isn't the whole sentence." She names it plainly — Lilianne used
the Fire Talkers' own weapon, the one Naiad warned her about the day
the Old Oak gave it to her, to end the war. Points out Sorrel once sat
at Lilianne's own table telling the truth at a cost, and was burned
without a chance for the dice to say no. Promises to finally finish
what she never told Lilianne about Drowned City.

**Lilianne:** "So much screaming. Men incinerated alive. Because of
me."

**Naiad:** Doesn't soften it. Reveals that Lilianne's father stood in
an identical silence after Drowned City, and that she told him nothing
useful then either — because there isn't anything useful to say. States
plainly that both things are true at once: Lilianne chose the weapon
that makes men scream, and she chose it because it was the only path to
the peace the camp is celebrating. Says her father eventually stopped
choosing fire, and that's the difference worth holding onto.

**Lilianne:** "Nothing's changed. I swore an oath of protection... What
good will it do that I still don't want it?"

**Naiad:** Won't pretend it undoes yesterday. Warns that soldiers who
stop asking that question get faster, not gentler, at starting the next
war — and that she watched this begin happening to Lilianne's father.
Tells her the oath's cruelty is that it can ask you to become, for one
battle, the thing you swore to protect against — and that still asking
the question afterward is what keeps a Chancellor trustworthy with
fire.

**Lilianne:** *(after a long silence)* "When will father be back, do
you think? ... Why did he leave? What's he doing out there?"

**Naiad:** States she doesn't know if he's alive — no proof either way.
Reveals: he left because of what he did *after* Drowned City, not the
battle itself — spent years hunting down surviving Fire Talkers, growing
quieter each time, until a year ago he stopped coming back from those
searches at all. Confirms his own hand, not just the Fire Talkers',
struck the killing blow on Isolde Varn — a fact history recorded under
their name instead of his.

**Lilianne:** "But why now? ... was he waiting for an heir to turn
eighteen so he could finally be rid of the title?"

**Naiad:** Flatly denies it — he never once treated raising Lilianne as
a countdown to his own release. Reveals the trigger: an unmarked letter
reached him a year ago, from someone claiming to have survived Drowned
City, finally answering a question he'd carried for fifty-nine years.
He left within the week. Naiad doesn't know what the letter said, only
that it's still "very much alive," whatever it is.

**Lilianne:** *(closing her eyes)* "Oh, father..." Recounts fearing
she'd kill Sorrel this battle despite orders to take her alive. Notes
the parallel to Corvin Varn, who allied with the Fire Talkers trying to
start a rebellion — says she understands his father's guilt a little
better now.

**Naiad:** Recognizes Corvin's name from reports — didn't know he'd
gone as far as courting the Fire Talkers directly. Credits Lilianne for
holding Sorrel back from the battlefield herself rather than repeating
her father's sixty years of silence. Encourages the instinct to
understand Corvin rather than just condemn him, calling it her father's
better half. Offers to help her up, promises to pull on the thread of
her father's disappearance once the peace holds.

**Lilianne:** *(rising, then pausing at the tent flap)* "I've made my
decision, mother. We'll bury the Flame. Let's end the cycle of
killing. We'll build a memorial of this battle right here, and bury
the fire next to the Giant. This place will be a symbol of peace, not
just a reminder of tragedy. And when it's done, I'll swear a new Oath.
I'll devote myself to the people, and their well-being."

**Naiad:** Names the real cost — giving up the weapon that just won the
war — but doesn't argue against it. Contrasts Lilianne's choice to bury
Sticky Fire openly with her father's choice to carry his own version of
it silently until it drove him away. Calls the new Oath naive in the
best possible sense — the only kind worth being Chancellor for. Kisses
her forehead, promises to arrange the masons and start drafting who
needs to hear about the new Oath before it's sworn.

**Lilianne:** "Goodnight, mother." Exhausted, but with some light
returned to her eyes.

**[Conversation concluded]**
