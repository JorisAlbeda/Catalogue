# Converse — Corvin & Perrin (Round 2, #1)

**Setting:** The mill at Fertile Valley, weeks after the second defeat. Perrin's word came by runner, not by challenge — a place, a time, nothing else written down.

**Perrin:** Two fights over the same stretch of dirt, and I still don't know your face without a line of my men between us — sit down, Corvin. I'm not looking to make it three.

**Corvin:** *(turns the signet ring once, sits anyway)* "Then don't. I didn't come to refight it — I came because you asked, and I don't like leaving a question unpaid any more than I like leaving ground unclaimed. So ask what you actually called me here for, Ashe. I doubt it's the dirt."

**Perrin:** *(glances toward the mill door before he answers)* Fair enough. You held this ground before I did — twice, between the two of us. There's a key inside that mill that used to open something worth opening. Did you ever hear, while it was yours, where the charter that key belonged to actually went?

**Corvin:** *(shakes his head slowly)* "No. Whatever that key used to open, I never heard a word of it — I had bandits to clear and a book of my own to close before I thought to ask what these walls used to guard. If it matters that much to you, that's the first honest thing you've told me since we sat down." *(a beat)* "My turn, then. You've beaten me twice over the same dirt. What is it you actually want from it, Ashe — the ground, or whatever that key was supposed to open?"

**Perrin:** *(quiet a moment, turning it over)* The ground held the mill. The mill held the key. I didn't stop to ask which one I was actually fighting for until you just made me. *(a short breath)* Ask me again once I've used the key on something.

**Corvin:** *(a short nod, no argument in it)* "Then I'll hold you to that instead of the dirt. Come find me when you have your answer, Ashe — I'm better at collecting on those than I ever was at holding ground anyway."
