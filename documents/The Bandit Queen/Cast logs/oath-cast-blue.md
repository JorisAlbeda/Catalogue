# Cast — Blue

*The roll of names Corvin's kept in the back of his own ledger, added to in his own hand whenever someone new sits at his fire, or finally shows him something they'd been keeping back.*

## Hesper (Memory of Home)

Keeper of the old way station on the Hinterland road, long before she kept anything of Corvin's. Showed him her whole face at last the evening before a trade at Tinker's Fair, done pretending she had nothing left to offer him. Can call every last coin out of one treasury and send it wherever the wandering folk keep their own — but the doing of it costs her a secret burned to nothing, and she never does it without flinching once. Refolds any scrap of cloth she's handed back into the same creases, every time, as though it still belongs to someone who's coming back for it.

## Ressa Kindler (Fire Talkers)

Sat at his table since the first days at Fertile Valley — of the same clan whose fire turned back two of Isolde Varn's own charges at Drowned City. Kept her reasons to herself for a long while before finally showing him her whole face, ahead of a trade at Magician's Code worth the risk. Can steady or ruin a man's luck by three turns either way in a fight, but only while the Darkest Secret is hers to hold, and only at the cost of a secret spent outright. Never raises her voice, but always sits facing the door, like she's still expecting someone to come collect an old debt in blood.

## Old Marta (Seer of Rebellion)

A wandering seer who found him at the Mine and told him a future he wasn't sure he wanted to hear. Corvin read her prophecy out loud himself rather than lose Hesper or Ressa to make room for anyone else, and it's stood open ever since, plain for anyone to hear: that he'd be named heir to the People's Favor outright, the morning he still held it with the land's omens already read three times over. He held that banner three separate times before the war's end. The morning never came. Answers every question with another question, as though the future were something you had to bargain your way toward rather than be told.

## Sergeant Colm Rooke (Battle Honors)

A soldier who swore, at the way station, that any real victory Corvin won would pay for itself. Showed his face plainly from the day he joined, no hesitation in it, and none since. Earns Corvin a purse from the old military treasury every time he wins a fight outright — no price asked of him for it, unlike the others at this table. Keeps a list of every commendation he was never actually given, and reads from it before any fight whether asked to or not.
