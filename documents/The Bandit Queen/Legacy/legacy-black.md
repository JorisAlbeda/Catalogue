# Legacy — Black

## Perrin Ashe

The Horned Mask never left the Imperial Reliquary. Offered three times to an Exile named Ashe — the last in his own grandfather's ruined hall — and refused each time, plain, no theater in it. The Chancellor let it sit rather than force it on anyone since, and a hundred years hasn't moved anyone to wear it. The ledger still carries the offer in a clerk's cramped hand, older than anyone currently drawing a wage there.

What isn't in that ledger: the man who said no walked out past the Hinterland with three provinces and a miller's daughter's key, and built a country called Ashvale that's answered to nobody but itself since. It's grown in the century since — its own borders, its own roads, its own harvests, and a ruling line that still keeps the story of the mask at their own table, though not the way the Empire tells it. The two nations have kept an uneasy peace more often than a war, neither one fully trusting the other's account of that morning at the ruined temple.

Whoever continues Black's legacy next should know this: the Mask is still in the Reliquary, still unclaimed, still asking what it asked once already — whether holding a symbol is the same as holding what it stood for. Halvard's own answer cost him a throne. His grandson's cost him nothing, and built a country instead. Whatever comes out of Ashvale this time, it won't be arriving as a stranger.
