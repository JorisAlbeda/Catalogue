# Legacy — White

## Sorrel Wick

A century on, nobody at Charming Valley can say for certain which desk was hers — the Truthwatcher's post changed hands so many times after her that the title outlasted the memory of any one person holding it. What survives isn't a record. It's a habit: clerks at that same desk still won't finish a half-heard accusation out loud before checking both the accused's hands, a superstition nobody can explain anymore except to say it's "the old custom," older than anyone currently keeping it.

One object remains, catalogued rather than displayed — a painting, smoke-darkened, of a woman in a plain dark coat facing a drawn blade with both hands open and empty. The ledger beside it only names her Wick, first name lost to a water stain, and records that she was Exiled twice, disappeared once, and that no death was ever formally entered against her name in the Empire's own rolls. Whether that's an oversight or the truest thing anyone ever recorded about her, the ledger doesn't say.

A rhyme children in the Provinces still half-remember claims she's the reason bandits at Narrow Pass grew polite. Nobody living can confirm it. Nobody's bothered to disprove it either.
