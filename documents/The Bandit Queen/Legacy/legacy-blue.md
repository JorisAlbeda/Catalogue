# Legacy — Blue

## Corvin Varn

A century on, Ashvale is a country in its own right, a green line on the Woodland Empire's own maps where the Hinterland used to be, the one Exile's claim that outlived the Exile who made it. Imperial court record calls Corvin Varn the exile who mended what Isolde broke, and paid for a marker at Drowned City that names no one and apologizes to no one — it only reads: SHE SAID IT, IN THE END. Ashvale's own histories barely mention Isolde at all. What they remember instead is the year a foreign Chancellor buried a stolen flame at Buried Giant and called it mercy — and the Fire Talkers, Ressa Kindler's own people, who never once agreed to call it that. Some in Ashvale name it plain heresy: a debt owed back to the clan it was taken from, put in the ground instead, without so much as asking them first. Whoever carries fire in their blood next inherits that grievance along with whatever's left of the Varn name — and gets to decide whether reclaiming it means digging, or something else entirely.

The signet ring left the family within a generation, sold off in a leaner year no one likes to name now. What survived instead is smaller and stranger: a scrap of grey-green cloth, sewn into the lining of an old coat passed hand to hand since, and a habit that came with it, never explained to whoever inherited it next — don't call a debt settled just because it's gone quiet.
