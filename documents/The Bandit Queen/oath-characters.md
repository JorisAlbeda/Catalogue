# Oath Characters

## Lilianne of the Woods (Purple, Chancellor)

- Pronouns: she/her
- Location: Deep Woods
- Physical description: Black hair, sharp blue eyes. Wrapped in a mantel that seems too large for her.
- Personality description: A fiery passion and a strong sense of justice. Determined to fill her father's shoes.
- Bonds: Tends to get into heated discussions with Sorrel, but values her advice when it comes down to it. Trusts Naiad as both mother and Commander, but caught her hiding something about the Drowned City and the war that made her father Chancellor — hasn't decided yet whether that was protection or a lie. Let Sorrel see her grieving her father openly for the first time at Drowned City, and heard her deny to her face that the People's Favor was ever about rebellion — didn't answer before Sorrel left, and it's still sitting with her. Offered Perrin Ashe everything a Cray had lost at Fertile Valley — the valley restored, a seat at her table, even the Sorcerer's own mask handed back as a gift — and watched him refuse all of it standing in his grandfather's own ruined hall. Named him an enemy before she left; didn't look like it cost her anything to say, though the refusal plainly told her something she'd come to learn. The night the war ended, finally heard the rest of what Naiad had been holding back since Round 0: her father's own hand struck the killing blow on Isolde Varn at Drowned City, not just the Fire Talkers he commanded; he spent the years since hunting down what was left of that clan out of guilt, and vanished a year ago within a week of an unmarked letter from someone claiming to have survived that battle. Naiad doesn't know if he's alive. Grieved openly in front of her mother for the men Sticky Fire killed at Buried Giant, and for the first time drew her own line against repeating his silence rather than his war. Met Corvin Varn unexpectedly at Drowned City on private business of her own, and gave him the one piece of Isolde's story her father had actually told her: that Isolde let Wren Sable take the blame for a crime that was hers, confessed only the day she died. Told plainly, in turn, that Sorrel doesn't believe there's anything left of the woman she grew up with under the Chancellor's mantle — advised to find out herself whether that's still true, now that the Flame's buried.
- Recent Interactions: Sorrel Wick — spoke at Drowned City about her father, the banner, and what Naiad still isn't saying; see `Game/Story/Conversation logs/oath-conversation-sorrel-lilianne-R4-1.md`. Naiad — shown the Verdflayme orb granted by the Old Oak; see `Game/Story/Conversation logs/oath-conversation-lilianne-naiad-R0-1.md`. Perrin Ashe — met at the Ruined Temple, offered restoration and a symbol, refused both; see `Game/Story/Conversation logs/oath-conversation-perrin-lilianne-R6-1.md`. Naiad — the night the war ended, grieved what Sticky Fire cost at Buried Giant, learned the truth of her father's disappearance, and decided to bury the Flame; see `Game/Story/Conversation logs/oath-conversation-lilianne-naiad-R9-2.md`. Corvin Varn — met at Drowned City, traded the last of what either knew about Isolde and Wren Sable; see `Game/Story/Conversation logs/oath-conversation-corvin-lilianne-R9-1.md`.
- Notes: Commands the Empire's strongest force, the Rangers, led by her mother Naiad. Carries a glass orb of Verdflayme (Sticky Fire), a Fire Talker's weapon granted by the Old Oak — bound for the Drowned City, where Naiad admits she knows things about the final battle she hasn't told Lilianne. Won the war and the game as Oathkeeper (5 Relics, both Banners) at Buried Giant, Round 8 — killed Sorrel's entire defending force with Sticky Fire after already winning the Campaign outright, and was shaken enough by it to decide, the same night, to bury the relic beside a memorial at Buried Giant and swear a new Oath devoted to the people's well-being once it's built.

## Sorrel Wick (White, Exile)

- Pronouns: she/her
- Location: Narrow Pass
- Physical description: Ink-stained fingers she never quite scrubs clean; keeps a scrap of grey-green cloth — inherited, unwashed, unexplained to anyone who asks — pinned inside her coat.
- Personality description: Blunt to the point of rudeness when it matters, otherwise watchful and quiet. Treats a locked ledger box like it owes her an explanation.
- Bonds: Wary of Lilianne (Purple, Chancellor)'s regime and of Naiad's secrets, but not of Lilianne herself — the two grew up together, and at Drowned City she finally said so plainly: she came back for Lilianne, not against her. Told her, for the first time, whose cloth she's actually carried since Wren Sable died. Banished by her at Buried Giant after Naiad caught her admitting the same plan out loud in front of Ossa, Fen Quill, and Marlow Ash — Lilianne looked like she meant to say something else at the very end, and didn't. Still meant what she told her at Drowned City. Hasn't decided whether Lilianne believed it. Banished a second time, months later, after Lilianne marched on Buried Giant herself with a force Sorrel had no answer for and put the whole garrison to the torch rather than just winning — no hesitation this time, no unfinished sentence, just the debt settled plainly. Reads it as confirmation there's nothing left of Lily under the Chancellor's mantle to hope for. Owes Daz (Narrow Pass) a debt of a different kind now — no coin asked, two of her own people sent along anyway, the first thing offered to Sorrel since the exile that wasn't a transaction. Epilogue: went to Perrin and Corvin at the Ruined Temple, two months into the truce, and got a second thing offered without a price attached — ground of her own in Ashvale, hers to pick or not. Finally handed Corvin the cloth she's carried since Wren Sable died and asked him straight whether Isolde's debt was ever paid; he told her the truth instead of comfort — not paid, but no longer sitting unclaimed either. Took the night to decide what she wants from either offer. Wrote Corvin a letter before leaving at dawn, retracting her own tidy answer and handing the real, harder question about Isolde back to him unresolved. Four months after the war: Corvin returned from Drowned City having met Lilianne there by chance — confirmed the harder truth about Isolde outright, and told Lilianne some of what Sorrel had said about her since the exile besides. Called him on sharing that without asking first, forgave it in the same breath she'd done worse to him with the cloth. Hearing that Lilianne called her own war a worse failure than her father's, and buried the fire, left something in Sorrel that isn't quite hope and isn't quite grief — hasn't decided what to do with it, and didn't say whether she wants Lilianne to come find her.
- Recent Interactions: Lilianne of the Woods — banished at Buried Giant, Naiad at her side; see `Game/Story/Conversation logs/oath-conversation-sorrel-lilianne-R5-1.md`. Daz — sat by her fire at Narrow Pass with Ossa, Fen Quill, and Marlow Ash beside her; see `Game/Story/Conversation logs/oath-conversation-sorrel-daz-R8-1.md`. Perrin Ashe & Corvin Varn — met at the Ruined Temple, offered a home in Ashvale; see `Game/Story/Conversation logs/oath-conversation-sorrel-perrin-corvin-R8-1.md`. Corvin Varn — heard out his account of Drowned City and Lilianne, four months after the war; see `Game/Story/Conversation logs/oath-conversation-sorrel-corvin-R9-1.md`.
- Notes: Inherited a sealed ledger box marked SANCTUARY, and the Truthwatcher's desk at Charming Valley, from Wren Sable — along with an unanswered question about whether Isolde Varn ever deserved her apology.

## Corvin Varn (Blue, Exile)

- Pronouns: he/him
- Location: Fertile Valley
- Physical description: Blue-grey skin like every Exile; broad-shouldered, close-cropped hair going prematurely grey at the temples; wears a signet ring too big for his hand, resized twice by someone else's jeweler.
- Personality description: Deliberate and stubborn, treats every open question like unfinished work. Doesn't perform grief, but won't let a debt lie unpaid just because it's inconvenient.
- Bonds: Isolde Varn's grandson — she died at Drowned City twenty-some years before he was born, so everything he carries of her is inherited, not remembered. Carries the Varn name forward from her — not chosen, inherited — and hasn't decided whether he owes the dead Chancellor or the wronged exile the greater debt. Sits uneasily but honestly with Ressa Kindler over the fire — her clan burned Isolde's charges back twice at Drowned City sixty years ago, and neither of them chose the debt they're each holding because of it; nothing's forgiven, but neither's turned the other away. Met Perrin Ashe at the mill after losing Fertile Valley to him twice — left it without rancor, holding Perrin to a promised answer about an old key instead of the ground itself. Once Perrin's own letter finally came, went to the mill himself to collect it in person, and stayed past the collecting — told him, unasked, whose ring he carries, and got the rest of what Perrin hadn't finished writing back in kind. That debt was the one that actually settled first, whatever else was still undecided. At the Ruined Temple, took Isolde's own cloth from Sorrel Wick's hands for the first time — Wren Sable's inheritance, unworn since — and gave her the honest answer instead of an easy one: the debt isn't paid, but it isn't sitting unclaimed anymore either. Sorrel's own letter the next morning walked that answer back further than either of them had let it go at the Temple: Wren's box never named Isolde owed an apology, only that he never decided if she deserved one — meaning whatever she confessed at Drowned City on the last day of her life may have been owed to him, not the reverse. Corvin's taking her at her word not to tidy that back up, and means to go stand at Drowned City himself, the first Varn to do so since Isolde — not to close it, just to stop being the only one who's never gone. A month later, found Lilianne of the Woods there on an errand of her own, and got the missing half from her plainly: Isolde let Wren Sable live as a criminal for years for a crime that was actually hers, and only said so the day she died. Didn't perform anger he had no right to. Left the debt heavier than he found it, and the question still open, same as Sorrel asked.
- Recent Interactions: Perrin Ashe — went to the mill himself, stayed the night; see `Game/Story/Conversation logs/oath-conversation-corvin-perrin-R8-1.md`. Sorrel Wick — took Isolde's cloth from her hands at the Ruined Temple, gave her a plain answer about the debt; see `Game/Story/Conversation logs/oath-conversation-sorrel-perrin-corvin-R8-1.md`. Lilianne of the Woods — met her at Drowned City a month later, traded the last of what either of them knew about Isolde and Wren Sable; see `Game/Story/Conversation logs/oath-conversation-corvin-lilianne-R9-1.md`.
- Notes: Took the Varn name as a young man, along with both halves of its unpaid debt. His first companion on the road was Hesper, keeper of an old way station, who calls the road home more than any roof. Won Fertile Valley and Boiling Lake from the bandits squatting there in the war's opening days, at the cost of two men, then lost rule of both within the same week to Perrin Ashe. Kept Ressa Kindler at his table despite her clan's history with Isolde Varn — learned from her the actual shape of Isolde's death: three charges thrown at Drowned City against the Sorcerer of the Woods, the Fire Talkers turning back the first two, the Sorcerer's own hand ending the third. Bought the Cursed Cauldron outright at the Mine's own price — a relic that lends a man's company strength for every enemy who falls before them — his first real prize of the war, on ground he never once ruled. Held the People's Favor three separate times before it passed for good into the Chancellor's own hands — taken from Sorrel, lost to Lilianne's fire at Charming Valley, taken back from Sorrel a second time, and lost again for the last time not long after. Spent most of the war rebuilding a company that kept getting broken out from under him, and never once stopped.

## Perrin Ashe (Black, Exile)

- Pronouns: he/him
- Location: Fertile Valley
- Physical description: Weathered travel-cloak gone the color of dust; sun-cracked hands; a thin scar over one eyebrow from a road he won't discuss.
- Personality description: Quietly stubborn — treats a closed door as a puzzle rather than a refusal. Slow to trust, faster to forgive than most give him credit for.
- Bonds: Returns to Bettina Marsh at the Fertile Valley mill whenever he needs the truth told plainly — she keeps Halvard Cray's old iron key on a nail there, and he can't yet explain why he checks it's still hanging every time he visits. Met Corvin Varn at the mill after their second fight over Fertile Valley — traded the ground's claim for an open question instead, and now owes him an answer about the key rather than owing him a fight. Met Lilianne of the Woods at the Ruined Temple, on his own ground — she offered him the valley restored, a seat at the Empire's table, and his grandfather's own conqueror's mask, and he refused all three standing where Halvard's throne used to be. She named him her enemy on her way out. Neither of them looked surprised by it. In the war's last stretch, paid the key's debt in full, then Corvin came to the mill himself to collect it in person and didn't leave after — said out loud, finally, what checking that nail every visit had actually been about. Whatever the next dawn decided, he'd rather sit it out with Corvin across from him than back at the mill alone. Months into the truce, as Warden of Ashvale, received Sorrel Wick at the Ruined Temple — the first debt he settled with her was simply not holding her silence against her, three unanswered messages and all — and offered her ground of her own to pick, no claim owed for it.
- Recent Interactions: Corvin Varn — came to the mill himself this time, stayed past the lamp going out; see `Game/Story/Conversation logs/oath-conversation-corvin-perrin-R8-1.md`. Lilianne of the Woods — met at the Ruined Temple, refused her offer, named an enemy; see `Game/Story/Conversation logs/oath-conversation-perrin-lilianne-R6-1.md`. Sorrel Wick — received her at the Ruined Temple as Warden of Ashvale, offered her a home; see `Game/Story/Conversation logs/oath-conversation-sorrel-perrin-corvin-R8-1.md`.
- Notes: Motivated by the charter that wandered out of Fertile Valley's keeping and turned up filed among a usurper's things — means to see it brought home, and to understand exactly which turn let the gate stop answering to its own Chancellor. Flaw: mistakes holding a place for holding what's inside it, the same misjudgment that cost Halvard the throne. Owed Corvin Varn an answer about the key, once he'd actually used it on something — a promise made at the mill, not the ground; paid in full by the war's end (see Bonds). Early in the war, took the Mine as his second stronghold. Not long after, declared his hidden ambition openly — to reclaim his grandfather's old conquest and rule more ground than anyone else — after reinforcing both Fertile Valley and the Mine heavily. At the time the Chancellor held just as much ground as he did, so the claim meant nothing yet; it would only matter once that changed, one way or the other. The same day, called every company back to Fertile Valley and said the charter's name aloud for the first time, naming it Halvard Cray's own war continued — Bettina Marsh watched from the mill door and didn't leave. Later, turned down the Chancellor's own offer of citizenship, a rebuilt Fertile Valley, and the Horned Mask her father wore the day he broke Halvard — learned, in the same breath, that "the Bandit Witch of the Narrow Pass" is the Empire's own name for Daz. Openly named an enemy of the Chancellor as of that meeting.
