# Narrative Log — Blue

## Round 1 — Corvin Varn

**Development.** Corvin reaches Fertile Valley to find it unclaimed and squatted by bandits, with Perrin Ashe already camped on the same ground.

**Discussion.** Hesper warned against throwing every warband into a first-day fight with nothing held back, and later that giving up the Traveling Doctor's insurance so soon after was a real cost, not a free discard; Corvin overruled her both times. Ressa Kindler, newly arrived at his table, offered only that three warbands hadn't been enough against bandits and wouldn't be enough against worse.

**Action.** Campaigned against the Bandits and won Fertile Valley — and, with it, Boiling Lake — at the cost of two warbands, then spent the rest of the day searching: kept Ressa Kindler on despite her clan's history with Isolde Varn, and set a facedown Vision of Sanctuary into the ground he'd just bled for.

## Round 1 — Corvin & Ressa, by the fire

A private table-talk, not a Converse (Ressa is Corvin's own adviser, not another seat's character) — logged here per the same closing discipline. Corvin asked her outright about the name he carries; Ressa told him plainly what her clan actually did at Drowned City sixty years ago — the fire that turned back two of Isolde Varn's charges, and the Sorcerer of the Woods's own hand on the third. Neither apologized. Neither left the fire. They settled on holding two debts neither of them chose, side by side, without pretending either one is settled.

## Round 2 — Corvin & Perrin, at the mill

Corvin came when Perrin called, more out of a dislike for unpaid questions than any real curiosity about the dirt between them. Learned nothing about the key or its charter — he'd never thought to ask what those walls used to guard while the ground was his — but left holding something else: Perrin's own word that he'll come back with an answer once that key's been used on something. Took the trade without complaint; collecting on a promise suits him better than holding ground ever did. Full exchange: `Game/Story/Conversation logs/oath-conversation-perrin-corvin-R2-1.md`.

## Round 2 — Corvin Varn

**Development.** Corvin has nothing left to spend at Fertile Valley — no relic, no open table at Boiling Lake, no debt collectible there that isn't already spoken for — and he's just told Perrin the ground doesn't interest him anymore anyway.

**Discussion.** Hesper worried that walking off bled-for ground so soon looks like surrender dressed up as indifference; Ressa Kindler cared less about appearances and more about the math — an empty field is only worth looking at, not holding, until warbands actually stand on it.

**Action.** Left Fertile Valley behind for the last unclaimed stretch of the Hinterland, spending half his Supply just to see what's under it — a mine, guarding a cauldron he couldn't yet afford and an empty stretch of ground beside it. Spent what remained talking to whoever the mine and its village would send his way, and came back with a keeper for the way station rather than the messenger or the mine-worker — someone who'd let him pass through free later, which mattered more to him than a single favor spent once. Rested there with nothing left to spend.

## Round 3 — Corvin Varn

**Development.** Someone else's warbands hold the ground around him, but the cauldron the mine's been sitting on is still just sitting there, unclaimed by anyone with the coin to take it.

**Discussion.** Hesper allowed that a real prize in hand beats another debt owed, watching him hand over the one secret he'd kept since Setup without complaint; Ressa's only worry was that it leaves him standing at someone else's site with empty pockets.

**Action.** Traded away the last thing he'd carried out of Setup for the coin to finally pay the cauldron's price, and walked out of the mine holding the first real prize of the whole campaign — then spent what was left seeing what else the road still had for him. Traffic through the way station brought two men he had no place left to seat, and one old woman who wouldn't say what she wanted until she had it — only that the land had another future worth knowing about, if he was willing to bargain for it. He took her on and sent the other two back the way they came — and let go, at last, of the sanctuary he'd folded into Fertile Valley's ground a lifetime of Supply ago, since holding it had never asked anything of him he wasn't already sworn to want.

## Round 4 — Corvin Varn

**Development.** The ground under Corvin's feet still belongs to Perrin, and a hard look at what's actually left in his own bank tells him this isn't the day that changes.

**Discussion.** Hesper talked him back from the mine's own garrison before he'd committed anything real — one warband thrown at four is a debt paid to nobody, not a fight. Old Marta, asked what she made of the day, only allowed that some ground is worth walking away from twice.

**Action.** Left the mine's rule to Perrin a second time and turned instead to the road ahead of him, spending what Supply he had left seeing what else the world still had to offer. Of the three who answered the way station's call, he sent away the wolf-tamer and the quiet envoy both, and kept only the soldier who swore a plain thing: that any real victory, when Corvin finally won one, would pay for itself. There was no room at his own table for a third stranger until he did what Old Marta's own words had already told him to do — said her future out loud instead of keeping it folded facedown in his coat, the same lesson he'd taken as agreement without realizing it was meant for her too. Before he left the way station, he had the soldier's own commendations read aloud for anyone to hear, and spent his last secret there besides — banking the favor it bought rather than the coin itself, with a use for it already in mind.

## Round 5 — Corvin Varn

**Development.** Two turns of banking finally add up to enough — a banner nobody's actually defended since the day it was taken, and Corvin holds just enough coin to take it in full view of the whole table.

**Discussion.** Ressa's whole assessment fit in two lines: a debt paid in full is still a debt, whoever's holding it now, and she'd rather he took it plainly than pretend it was owed to him some other way.

**Action.** Paid above the People's Favor's own standing price and took it from Sorrel Wick outright, spending every coin he had to do it, then sent word of it ahead by his own soldier rather than let her find the empty space herself. Rode on to Charming Valley with nothing left in his purse, let Ressa show her own face at last for the trade it earned him, and put the valley's open tables to work — one trade, one muster — until idle warbands finally became a real force again.

## Round 6 — Corvin Varn

**Development.** Lilianne walked into Charming Valley and took back in an afternoon what took Corvin three rounds to build — the banner, every warband he'd just raised, gone in the same visit that cost Sorrel her own citizenship.

**Discussion.** Hesper didn't waste breath mourning what was already lost — nothing left here worth the one Relic Corvin still has, not with the Chancellor's own pawn still standing in the same yard.

**Action.** Sent word to Sorrel that he'd noticed what she'd already answered better than he had — then, with the round running short, let Hesper show her own face at Tinker's Fair and took the banner back from Sorrel the same afternoon she'd taken it back from him. Left before anyone else got a turn at what little else he had, and found the pass itself standing in the way of anywhere further he meant to go.

## Round 7 — Corvin Varn

**Development.** The banner's gone again — Lilianne pried it loose the same week she took it from him the first time — and word's reached him that Sorrel's found something at Buried Giant worth more to her than anything he's still holding.

**Discussion.** He's handing over the last secret they're keeping right there at the pass to the dazzling woman who mans it, for whatever coin she'll give him, meaning to carry every bit of it into Perrin's valley and spend it there putting new hands on his shoulders again — because a man standing at Narrow Pass with no one behind him is just a man waiting to be found alone.

**Action.** Traded away the last whisper he'd carried since the Mine right there at the pass, then walked on into Perrin's own valley without asking leave and spent the coin it bought him calling two fresh hands to his side. Sent Colm Rooke ahead of him besides — not for Perrin's sake alone, but because Sorrel's found a prize at Buried Giant that ends the whole game the moment she wakes to it again, and Perrin's the only one standing close enough to still do something about it before then.

## Round 8 — Corvin Varn

**Development.** The Chancellor's taken everything worth taking this week — the banner from him, then Sorrel's whole standing and her own prize besides — and there's no coin left in the world that buys back a day like this one.

**Discussion.** The Chancellor holds the people's love in her fist now, out of reach, and no one at this table pretends that isn't her day coming — but building himself back into something that can stand still means something, brick by brick, rather than waiting for the reckoning empty-handed.

**Action.** Traded the last whisper he had left at the old bubbling lake — the ruined temple beside it wanted nothing to do with him — then walked on to the mine his people once eyed and called two fresh hands to his side there, four standing behind him now instead of two, whatever that ends up being worth. Sent Colm Rooke ahead one more time, not with a warning this time, but to ask Perrin, plainly, for the answer about the key he's owed since the mill — in case there's no later day left to collect it.

## Round 8 — Corvin & Perrin, at the mill

Corvin went himself this time instead of sending Colm Rooke again, and didn't leave before Perrin finished the part of the letter he hadn't actually written down. Told him, unprompted, whose ring he's actually been carrying all war — traded a plain answer for a plainer one back. Neither of them left before the lamp did. ## Epilogue — The Ruined Temple

Stood beside Perrin at the temple Sorrel picked herself, two months into the truce, and answered for the room before Perrin had finished getting to it — told her plainly there was space in Ashvale for her, same as there'd been for him, twice over, when he had nothing either. Took the cloth she finally held out — the first plain word either of them's had about Isolde in longer than he can count — and gave her the honest answer instead of a kind one: the debt isn't paid, but it isn't sitting unclaimed anymore. Let Perrin close the night out with the mill's own spare room and didn't argue the offer. Full exchange: `Game/Story/Conversation logs/oath-conversation-sorrel-perrin-corvin-R8-1.md`.

## Epilogue — Sorrel's letter

Read it twice before sitting down with it, the cloth already out of his coat before he'd decided to take it out. She'd taken back the tidy version she gave him at the Temple: Wren Sable's own box never called Isolde owed an apology, only that Wren never decided if she deserved one — meaning whatever she said aloud on the last day of her life at Drowned City may have been owed to *him*, not the other way round. Sorrel asked him plainly not to tidy that back up just because open is harder to carry than settled. Weighed the letter down on the mill table with the grain scale instead of folding it away, and said as much out loud, to Perrin or to no one. Means to go to Drowned City himself now — not to close the question, since she asked him not to, but because no Varn's actually stood on that ground since Isolde did, and he's done being the only one who's never gone to listen.

## Epilogue — Drowned City

A month's ride south of Ashvale, back across the Narrow Pass and into ground he'd never once crossed while it still answered to the Empire. Found the city itself nothing like the painting on his own wall — gulls instead of ghosts, no smoke anywhere he could find it — and found Lilianne of the Woods there besides, two days into some private errand of her own. Sat with her over tea rather than past history, and came away with the missing half of the debt: Isolde let Wren Sable live as a criminal for years rather than answer for a crime she'd actually committed herself, and only said so out loud the day she died. Didn't perform anger he didn't feel a right to. Told Lilianne plainly what Sorrel had said of her, and pointed her toward finding out herself whether it was still true. Left still wearing the ring and the cloth both, the question weighed down rather than folded shut, same as Sorrel asked.
