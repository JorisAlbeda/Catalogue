# Narrative Log — White

## Round 0

- **Development** — Word reached Charming Valley before Sorrel had even unpacked: the Chancellor was home from Drowned City with both hands full, and the desk that inherited Wren's old debts suddenly looked very small next to that.
- **Discussion** — Ossa didn't bother softening it: chasing paperwork while Lilianne came home laden was a coward's excuse wearing patience as a disguise. Sorrel let him finish, then told him plainly that this desk was never going to win a race measured in relics, and she wasn't going to waste the morning pretending otherwise.
- **Action** — She picked up a new hand at the desk, too — a light-fingered sort named Fen Quill, who tested the inkwell for loose coins before he'd even said hello — and spent the rest of the day making Charming Valley harder to lie to: a standing bargain with the traveling fair that no one could quietly wriggle out of later, a favor bought plainly off the magician's stall instead of tricked out of him, and word put out that nobody hostile to her would find help from the town's own people. Slower work than a relic hunt. She went to bed sure it was the right kind of slow.

## Round 2

- **Development** — The ledger showed the gap plainly: no Favor banked, Lilianne four Relics deep, and the one prize that's actually Sorrel's to win — the People's Favor itself — still sitting unclaimed somewhere out past her own desk.
- **Discussion** — Ossa argued she was hedging against a risk that wasn't real, since Tinker's Fair and Forest Council are already open for anyone to read; Sorrel didn't disagree with the logic, just wasn't ready to spend a one-time outburst on a guess she hadn't confirmed with her own eyes. When what came back from Corvin's own leavings split the desk down the middle — Ossa wanted her to gut Magician's Code for a lock with nothing yet to lock, Fen Quill called that the exact same unconfirmed guess she'd just declined to make on him — she sided with the caution again.
- **Action** — Traded her last secret at Tinker's Fair for a favor toward the banner, brought Fen Quill into the open at last, and went looking through what Corvin Varn had already thrown away rather than gamble on the wider world. Kept a quiet reader of leftovers named Marlow Ash out of it, sent the fortune-teller and the grave-digger back to the pile — then looked twice at the favor she'd told herself to hold onto, decided idle wasn't the same as saved, and spent it on Forest Council instead, two more companies behind the word she'd already put out. With the desk itself tapped dry and nine companies enough to hold it without her, she left Charming Valley in their care and walked south alone to Drowned City — the same ground the Chancellor's column had just picked clean — meaning to keep drawing on what it owed long after Lilianne's own attention moved elsewhere.

## Round 3

- **Development** — The People's Favor still sat where it had all game, untouched and undervalued at a single coin — the one prize actually written into Sorrel's own claim on this Oath, and nobody at the table had thought to reach for it yet.
- **Discussion** — Ossa and Fen Quill both said the same thing from different directions: the math left no room for error, and she'd end the day broke and exposed, holding something worth taking from her with her own people too far off to stop it. Marlow Ash agreed, and said it anyway — a full purse sitting idle was never the honest kind of safety.
- **Action** — She drew what Drowned City owed her — a secret, not the coin she'd expected — walked home, and fed the desk both secrets she had, one at each table, for the coin to close the gap. Took the People's Favor with both hands before anyone else at the table thought to want it.

## Round 4

- **Development** — The banner still carried the weight she'd put on it taking it home — two favors deep, not the bare thing she'd feared — and the morning's first debt was to bleed one of them off to whoever's coffers ran thinnest, not to fill anything further.
- **Discussion** — Ossa argued for banking every coin the desk earned rather than spending it on new blood or another road, certain the banner still needed shoring up. Once the ledger actually showed what the banner was already carrying, his caution didn't fit the numbers anymore, and she told him so.
- **Action** — Two secrets crossed the desk at Magician's Code and Forest Council, and she spent what they bought on fresh hands at Tinker's Fair — then thought better of marching them all home, keeping most of her strength close at hand instead of behind Charming Valley's own gate, so it would follow wherever she actually needed it rather than sit waiting on ground nobody was coming for. Only one company stayed to mind the desk itself. She turned south for Drowned City to keep that road paying out, and rested there with the ledger nearly bare.
- **Converse** — Found Lilianne camped in the tower over the water and went up uninvited. Told her, for the first time, whose cloth she's carried since Wren Sable died, and denied the banner was ever about rebellion to her face rather than through a note. Full dialogue: `Game/Story/Conversation logs/oath-conversation-sorrel-lilianne-R4-1.md`.

## Round 5

- **Development** — All that thought spent on where to put her companies, and none of it was ever actually holding the banner — Corvin Varn had simply been counting coins the whole time, and had enough of them now.
- **Discussion** — Ossa made her sit with an uncomfortable truth before she let herself believe there was any comeback at all: a claim like that only wins at its holder's own next dawn, not the moment the banner changes hands. Marlow put it plainer than either of them — she'd built a wall around something that was only ever a purchase, and should've been reading what Corvin threw away instead of watching her own gate.
- **Action** — Rather than trudge home to her own desk, she walked straight into Lilianne's own new hall at Buried Giant instead — Fen Quill and Marlow Ash both count as friends of the same gossiping circle the Chancellor had just sat down there, and neither of them cared whose banner it answered to. Two secrets became four favor before she'd so much as unpacked. Then, going through what the Provinces had thrown away, she found a rabble-rouser worth more to her than the rider Lilianne had already seated — and simply had the rider shown out, the banner in her own hands still carrying enough weight that nobody in the room thought to argue. She left it all sitting untouched in the strongbox.

## Round 6

- **Development** — Yesterday's Chancellor's own hall, and today Sorrel herself is still standing in it — banished, given three days to clear out, and finding nobody had actually bothered to leave a garrison behind to enforce that deadline. Nothing at Buried Giant was ever hers to reclaim; it was never anyone's alone.
- **Discussion** — Ossa didn't waste breath on the fight itself — the bandits themselves were never going to be the hard part. What he made her sit with was what came after: taking ground means nothing if she can't hold it against Lilianne coming back with real troops instead of a scattered few. Half measures don't survive that visit. She went in heavier than planned.
- **Action** — Campaigned against the Bandits holding Buried Giant with seven of her nine companies, lost two to the fight, and planted the five survivors on the ground itself — the first site that's answered to her alone since the exile. With Riots still sitting empty at the table, one more visit turned a secret into three favor, Fen Quill and Marlow Ash's own gossip still worth exactly what it was last round. With coin still in hand after resting once, she reached past Lilianne entirely and bought the People's Favor back outright — the very banner she'd been cast out for holding, back in her own two hands the same day, on ground that now answers to her and no one else. Spent what strength and coin remained combing the wider world instead of the Provinces' own bare shelves — a piper turned up first, but Perrin's own Ruined Temple still says no to anything with fur or claws, hers included. Looked hard at who else might be persuaded to join her and decided nobody currently sitting at her table was worth trading away for a stranger. Came home with nothing on that front, and nothing left to spend besides.

## Round 7

- **Development** — The banner had already moved twice more since she'd last held it, and by the time word reached her, it was sitting in Lilianne's own hands at a price nobody her size could meet. Whatever ground she'd clawed back a round ago wasn't going to be enough to catch that lead honestly.
- **Discussion** — Ossa wouldn't let her declare anything sight unseen — a claim like that becomes common knowledge the moment it's spoken aloud, and handing a rival the very thing that could beat her to the crown was worse than holding nothing at all. Read it first, he said, then decide.
- **Action** — Between messengers, the fever took her without warning — a song underneath everything, singing of secrets nobody had ever bothered to guard. She came out of it with the last and strangest claim to the throne still unmade: that whoever held the realm's darkest secret held the truest right to rule. It had sat untouched the whole war, waiting for nobody in particular. She declared it outright and walked straight to claim the secret backing it, paying more than its idle worth without a second thought. Come her own next dawn, if nothing else changes first, the crown is hers.

## Round 8

- **Development** — Someone talked, and Lilianne didn't come to argue. She came with an army Sorrel had no answer for and Verdflayme's own green fire, and she didn't stop at winning. The garrison broke in one pass; the ones who survived that didn't survive what came after — she burned them anyway, every last one of her people, the same ground Sorrel had taken with her own hands three days into exile. That wasn't a fight won. That was a message: nothing gets rebuilt here. And this time there was no hesitation first, no half-finished sentence at the door — just the banishment, plain and total, the way you'd settle any other debt. Sorrel had told her once, at Drowned City, that she'd never put her faith in anyone being strong enough forever, not Lilianne, not herself. She hadn't expected to be proven wrong quite this thoroughly, or to be the one it got proven on.
- **Discussion** — Ossa didn't let her sit in it. Attacking Perrin's ground for nothing would cost her the one honest ally she had left, and there was no relic or banner within reach worth the cost in her own people. But he wouldn't let "it's over" stand unchallenged either — Lilianne had just spent everything she had to make that strike, and nine companies sitting idle while there's still daylight left was its own kind of surrender. Told her to look before she rested.
- **Action** — Looked, and found nothing reachable — everything worth taking sat further than the day's own strength could carry her. Nothing closer belonged to anyone worth burning a bridge over. Turned instead to what the pass still owed her: the same three faces she'd already turned away once, the piper still no one's to keep, and neither of the other two worth trading Ossa, Fen Quill, or Marlow Ash's seat away for. Gathered two fresh hands to her side with what little was left, and settled in for the night with nothing to show for the day but a quieter kind of stubborn — no banner, no ground, no crown, Ossa, Fen Quill, and Marlow Ash the same three she started with, and whatever was left of Lily buried a little deeper under the Chancellor than it had been that morning.
- **Converse** — Went to Daz's own door at the pass's narrowest point with Ossa, Fen Quill, and Marlow Ash in tow, and got both a plain accounting of what the day had cost her and an unasked-for hand out of it — Daz sent two of her own people along, calling it neither pity nor a favor. Full dialogue: `Game/Story/Conversation logs/oath-conversation-sorrel-daz-R8-1.md`.

## Epilogue — The Ruined Temple

- **Converse** — Two months after the truce, found her way to Fertile Valley and picked the meeting ground herself: the Ruined Temple, the one corner of Ashvale nobody had claimed yet. Asked Perrin and Corvin plainly what she was walking into, arriving with nothing left to rule and no wish to rule anything again. Got an honest answer from both — a place, no strings, hers to pick or leave standing. Before deciding, finally held out the cloth she's carried unexplained since Wren Sable died and asked Corvin whether Isolde's own debt was ever actually settled; he took it and told her the truth — not paid, but not sitting unclaimed anymore either. Took the night to decide, same as she'd asked for, and slept in the mill's own spare room instead of turning back around. Full dialogue: `Game/Story/Conversation logs/oath-conversation-sorrel-perrin-corvin-R8-1.md`.
- Wrote Corvin a letter before leaving at dawn, taking back the tidy answer she'd given him at the Temple — Wren's own note only made sense if Isolde owed him the debt, not the other way round, and she wasn't going to let the easier version stand just because it sat better. Handed the real question back to him unresolved, the way Wren had left it to her. Delivered via `Game/Story/Messengers/oath-messengers-blue.md`.

## Round 9 — four months after the war

- **Converse** — Corvin rode south chasing that letter and came back three months late with more than he went looking for: Lilianne herself, met by chance at Drowned City, confirming the harder truth outright — Isolde let Wren carry the blame for a crime that was hers, and only confessed it dying, when it cost her nothing anymore. He'd also told Lilianne some of what Sorrel had said about her since the exile, and pushed her toward finding out for herself whether it still held. Called him on sharing what was hers to keep private, thanked him for the truth about Isolde regardless, and admitted she didn't know what to do with the idea of Lilianne cracking open enough to call her own war a worse failure than her father's. Didn't say whether she wants Lilianne to come. Didn't say she doesn't, either. Full dialogue: `Game/Story/Conversation logs/oath-conversation-sorrel-corvin-R9-1.md`.
