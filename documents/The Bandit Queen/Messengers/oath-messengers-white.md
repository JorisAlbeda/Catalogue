# Messengers — White

The messenger bows, humbly offering a sealed envelope from Her Excellency herself.
"To Our Loyal Subject, Sorrel Wick, whom we hold in high regard, these words we write as Chancellor of the Cradled Woods and Valleys:

Heeeey girl! What am I hearing about you recruiting armies everywhere for the Empire and getting people riled up under a single banner? Didn't take you for such a military strategist. What's your plan?

Signed with the Royal Seal.
Blessings of the wind be with you,
Chancellor Lilianne of the Woods."

It's the tone of voice Lily usually uses with you. The messenger still seems embarassed. "Her Excellency requests you respond with all urgent haste, my lady," she says.

## Round 6 — from Perrin Ashe (Black), via Fenn

Fenn ties Perrin's message to a strip of red wool — the same color as the banner cloth itself — around a spare wheel-spoke and hands it to a drover heading for Sorrel's camp, with instructions to leave it wedged in the fence post nearest her fire, no rider, no face to remember. Before the wagon rolls he counts the wheels twice under his breath and mutters that this one had better arrive before Corvin's morning does. The message: "Sorrel — we've never met, but the Chancellor's made enemies of us both in the same week, so I'll spend a moment on you I might otherwise spend guarding my own door. Corvin's promise comes due the moment his own morning comes, and yours falls right before it. If that banner's still in his hands when his morning comes, it's over for the rest of us, whatever we're each still building toward. I don't say this out of love for you or spite for him — only that I'd rather see this decided by all of us still standing, not settled quietly before anyone else gets a say."

## Round 7 — from Perrin Ashe (Black), via Fenn

Fenn slips back through the camp's edge before the second watch, finds Sorrel still by the low fire where the first message had reached her, and presses Perrin's word into her hand anyway: the Chancellor moved first, Corvin's promise is broken for now, the banner's back where it's always been, and no fire needs feeding this morning after all. The message: "Sorrel — the Chancellor beat us both to it. Corvin's promise is broken for now, so whatever else you were weighing this morning, that particular fire's out. But the banner's back in her hands, not stuck out of reach for good, and you've shown twice you know how to pry it loose. I don't tell you what to do with that. I just didn't want you spending anything on a threat that's already passed."

## Round 7 — from Perrin Ashe (Black), via Fenn (again)

Fenn counts the wheels twice before this one — a third trip up the same track in a week — and finds Sorrel by the cookfire mending a bridle, waiting for her hands to still before he speaks plain. The message: "Sorrel — plainer this time, since we're both short on time. I hold the whole Hinterland now, and it still isn't enough; the Chancellor's own household holds more ground than I do, and that's the whole difference between where I stand and where I mean to end up. You've already shown you can pry ground loose from her when it's worth your while. I'm asking you to do it again, somewhere that suits you — not for my sake, but because every stretch you take from her is one she doesn't have anymore, and I don't see how that costs you a thing. I won't ask twice, and I won't hold it against you if the answer's no."
