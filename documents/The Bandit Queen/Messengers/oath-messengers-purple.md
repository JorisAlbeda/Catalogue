# Messengers — Purple

Fen Quill slips into the Chancellor's hall unannounced, the sealed letter held loosely between two fingers as if he'd rather palm it than hand it over, and his eyes flick once across the table's scattered rings and unattended purses before he finally sets Sorrel's reply before Lilianne. He waits just a beat too long at her elbow while she breaks the seal, close enough to read the words upside-down, before stepping back with the easy, empty-handed grin of a man checking whether anyone noticed what he almost took.

"Not an army, Lily — a roof. Charming Valley just got a lot more people who'd rather it stayed standing than see it torn down for someone else's ledger. No plan beyond that, and no apology for it either."

## Round 6 — from Perrin Ashe (Black), via Fenn

Fenn writes Perrin's answer small and plain on a strip of oilcloth, seals it with plain gray wax rather than any house mark, and sends the sparrow back up off the Ruined Temple's broken sill. The message: "I'll meet you at the Ruined Temple, on my own ground, at the time you named. I've seen already what your crown can do to men who cross you — I'd like to hear, from you, why I shouldn't count myself one of them. Come with fewer soldiers than the ones you sent to burn mine."

## Round 7 — from Perrin Ashe (Black), via Fenn — URGENT

Fenn arrives at the Chancellor's own gate unescorted, wheels counted twice under his breath before he'll let his boots touch her stones, and hands the sealed word not to a steward but to Lilianne's own hand, refusing the offered chair until she's broken the seal herself. He speaks Perrin's message plain, without softening it or begging pardon for the enmity between them. The message: "Chancellor — I know exactly what I am to you now, and I'm not writing to change it. Sorrel Wick means to end this game outright at her own next dawn, on a claim she's already made good on. I have no road to reach her in time and nothing that would matter if I did. You do. I'm not asking anything for this — you can call it a debt if it suits you, or call it nothing at all. I'd simply rather lose to you on a board that's still standing than watch it taken off the table by someone else's omen while the rest of us weren't looking."
