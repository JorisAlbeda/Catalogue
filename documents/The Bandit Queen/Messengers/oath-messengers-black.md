# Messengers — Black

## From Corvin Varn, via Ressa Kindler

Ressa found Perrin at the mill again, facing the door the whole time she spoke rather than him. "Corvin heard what you named at Fertile Valley — Halvard Cray's war, not yours alone anymore. He's not come to collect on that key, not yet, but a war has a way of spending everything it's given, and he means to still be owed his answer when it does." Then, almost as an afterthought on her way out: "If it's a war you want, there's a chancellor with five stolen treasures and six conquered holds who'd give you a better one than any impoverished Exile would."

## Round 5 - from Lilianne of the Woods (Purple), via a sparrow.

Rumours have spread throughout the Hinterland about the Chancellor catching her strongest ally planning a coup, and exiling her. The road to the Cradle is long, and details are foggy - but one day, a carrier sparrow arrives with a note, sealed with the Chancellor's Seal. "I wish to speak to you. Let us meet by the Ruined Temple in one week's time. Send your reply with this sparrow."

## From Corvin Varn, via Sergeant Colm Rooke, with a warning

Colm finds Perrin at the edge of the Hinterland camp, hunched over a supply crate he's using as a map table, still scraping dried mud off his boot heel with a knife while his scouts break down the last watch-fire; he plants himself in front of the flame-light so Perrin has to look up, clears his throat like he's announcing something official, and recites the thing plain, the way a man says something he means to get exactly right. The message: "Corvin bids me tell you plain: Sorrel Wick has laid hands on the Darkest Secret and found the old claim bound to it — one that names her rightful ruler outright, clean, the moment her own dawn comes round again. He's got no road to reach her and no means to pry it loose himself in time, so the telling falls to you. Your next chance is the last one anyone gets before her own next dawn. He's not telling you what to do about it — only that you're the one standing close enough to do something, and he thought you'd rather know than not."

## From Corvin Varn, via Sergeant Colm Rooke, with a question

Colm finds Perrin at the edge of a burned grain terrace, kneeling by a supply cart with a broken axle, hammering the pin back through the wheel hub himself rather than waiting on a smith — he doesn't look up until Colm's close enough that his shadow crosses the wood. Perrin wipes his hands on his coat, waits for Colm to catch his breath, and only then asks what Corvin wants, like he'd already half-expected this errand before the day was out. The message: "Perrin — Corvin sends no demand and no soldiers, only me, and only this. Back at the mill in Fertile Valley, you told him that once you'd finally used that old iron key on something, you'd give him a straight answer about it — and you've spent this whole war since taking ground and never once brought it up. He isn't owed gold or ground for it, and he isn't angry, but if tomorrow is the reckoning for both of you, he doesn't want to go into it with that still sitting open between you. So he's asking you plainly, one last time, man to man — what did that key open, and what did you find."
