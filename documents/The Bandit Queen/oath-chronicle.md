# Oath Chronicle

## Round 0

Lilianne led the Rangers out of Deep Woods with the Old Oak's own blessing, trading favors for secrets that bought her Sticky Fire from the mill-ruins before she pushed on to the Drowned City for a Cracked Horn pulled from a doorway shut eighteen years. She made camp among the ruins with her stores nearly bare, and let Naiad's Rangers fill them back up.

Sorrel spent her first morning at the Truthwatcher's desk building nothing flashy — a light-fingered new hand named Fen Quill, a standing trade at Tinker's Fair, and a plain word from Forest Council that no enemy of Charming Valley would find help there. Ossa called it flinching in the face of the Chancellor's head start; Sorrel called it Tuesday.

## Round 1

Corvin drove the bandits off Fertile Valley at the cost of two warbands, then sat a woman named Ressa Kindler at his table — of the same clan that killed Isolde Varn sixty years before — and folded a Vision of Sanctuary facedown into the ground he'd just won.

Perrin let Corvin do the bleeding, then took Fertile Valley from him outright, sacrificing a warband to break the tie. He kept his own Vision of Conquest hidden among his advisers and made camp on ground better held than it had been that morning.

## Round 2

The Drowned City gave up one more secret before Lilianne moved on to Buried Giant, prying loose a fourth relic, the Ivory Eye, then pushed on once more to a place called Wastes and planted a Welcoming Party on its empty ground. She rested with her chest four relics heavier than when she'd set out.

Sorrel traded her last secret for a favor, brought Fen Quill fully into the light, and went hunting through what Corvin had already thrown away rather than gamble on the wider world — coming home with a quiet reader of leftovers named Marlow Ash. With nothing left to spend at her own desk, she left nine warbands to hold Charming Valley without her and walked south alone to Drowned City, meaning to keep drawing on what the ruin owed long after the Chancellor's attention moved elsewhere.

Corvin walked away from Fertile Valley with nothing left on it worth his Supply, crossing into the last unclaimed stretch of Hinterland to find a mine locked behind a price he couldn't meet and a village still deciding what it owed him. He came away with the keeper of a way station rather than the mine's own secrets — free passage later over one coin now.

Word reached Perrin that Corvin's road had ended at an empty mine, and against Fenn's own sharp warning, he left Fertile Valley in his warbands' keeping and crossed the Hinterland alone to see what still waited there. Three men had come with him after all, not one — he sent them all in, paid the same toll he'd paid at Fertile Valley to break another tie, and planted both survivors on the Mine itself. He traded his last secret at a way station Corvin had built and never used, and made camp holding two pieces of ground where a season ago he'd held none.

## Round 3

Lilianne pushed out of the Provinces and into the Hinterland, where the road itself stood guard before anyone else did — a defile so narrow it named itself, Narrow Pass, the only way through to the rest of the Hinterland from this side, and a relic held somewhere in its stone. She searched the pass for allies, and found a Giant Python, coiled faceup among her advisers now — anyone who brings a fight to her here will be hard pressed just to get past it. She made camp in the pass with three warbands at her back.

Sorrel came home from Drowned City with a secret instead of the coin she'd gone for, and spent everything the desk had anyway — both secrets, then every favor they bought — to take the Banner of the People's Favor with both hands before anyone else thought to want it. Ossa called it rich for exactly one day; Sorrel called it the only prize actually worth being poor for.

Corvin gave up the one thing he'd carried since reaching adulthood — a single secret, kept close since the day he took the Varn name — to buy his way past the cauldron's price at the mine that still wasn't his to rule. He walked out holding it anyway, the first real prize of his whole campaign, and turned what little he had left toward the road ahead. It brought him an old woman who spoke only in maybes, offering him a different future than the one he'd envisioned at Fertile Valley — he took her on and turned the rest away.

Perrin took the mine's own standing offer of a coin, then spent it and more calling fresh hands to both the mine and the valley before he'd let himself think about what came next. Only once both were thick with his own men did he announce his dream to the world - a return to a world of Conquest. And so, Perrin's war began. He would finish what his grandfather, Halvard Cray, once started.

## Round 4

Lilianne found a dazzling stranger waiting in Narrow Pass and gave her a seat at the table, trading a secret for the favor to afford what came next: a Bandit Crown, pried loose for three favors more, the fifth relic in her chest — and word ran ahead of her that the Empire now answered for six sites in all. She turned south again, back to the Drowned City with barely a supply left to her name, and let the ruin's own patience feed her before she rested.

Sorrel traded two secrets for favor, mustered fresh hands at Tinker's Fair, then thought better of marching them home — kept her strength close at hand instead, one lone warband left to mind Charming Valley's own gate — before turning south to keep Drowned City paying out.

Corvin looked hard at what he actually had left to throw at the mine and found it wasn't enough to call a fight — one warband against four is a debt paid to nobody. He let Perrin keep it a second time and turned back to the way station instead, where three more strangers had gathered waiting on his word. There was no seat left for any of them until he said out loud what Old Marta had been carrying facedown since she arrived — a future built on a banner he doesn't yet hold. With her words finally spoken instead of folded away, he sent the wolf-tamer and the quiet envoy back the way they came, and kept only the soldier who'd promised that the next real victory would pay for itself.

Corvin paid above the People's Favor's own asking price and took it from Sorrel outright, spending every coin to do it, then sent word ahead so she'd hear it from his own man before she saw the empty space where it used to sit. Purse empty, he rode on to Charming Valley and put its open tables to work instead — one trade, one muster — until idle warbands finally became a real force again.

Perrin left three at Fertile Valley to mind the mill and marched two out past every border he'd ever held, toward the bandits' own fire at Narrow Pass — the same fight he'd already won twice, and the first time he'd gone looking for it rather than stumbled into it. It wasn't the same fight after all: a stolen crown had made the bandits the Empire's own to defend, and the Empire's own green fire answered for them. Perrin walked home two companies lighter, the pass still bandit ground — though not before their own chief traded with him like nothing had happened an hour before.

## Round 5

Drowned City gave up its last secret to Lilianne before she left it behind for good, pushing on to Buried Giant to comb through what the Provinces had already discarded. Twice she turned the pile over — a hooded go-between named Gossip and a rider mid-gallop both found a place at Buried Giant's table, paying out in favor rather than blood. What she didn't keep, she fed to the bottom of the world deck itself through Cracked Horn's own quiet trick, leaving nothing behind for the next hand to find in the Hinterland's own pile. She made camp there with barely a supply to her name, and let it be enough.

Sorrel realized, too late, that the warbands she'd spent two rounds stacking at Charming Valley had never actually been guarding the one thing she couldn't afford to lose — the banner was only ever a purchase, and someone else had already saved up to buy it. Rather than go home, she walked straight into Lilianne's own hall at Buried Giant, where two familiar gossips of hers talked two secrets into four favor before she'd even sat down — then had one of Lilianne's own new guests shown the door and a sharper-tongued one seated instead, the banner still enough authority to make that stick. She let all five favor sit untouched in the strongbox, the first installment of whatever it would cost to buy the banner back the moment it changed hands.

Perrin went back to Narrow Pass meaning not to fight — bought two men off the same bandit chief who'd nearly burned him alive, kept them apart from Fertile Valley's own line, and walked them home with a fuller purse besides.

## Round 6

The realm held steady when Lilianne put the question to it at dawn — no ending written into this turn. She marched on Charming Valley itself, where Corvin sat holding the very banner Sorrel had lost him her own claim over, and answered it with green fire: two warbands paid down to break the tie, and a single favor handed to Corvin himself as the price of Sticky Fire's own appetite, which left none of his standing when the smoke cleared. She took the People's Favor with both hands and let it fall to its harder Mob side, watching two of its three favors burn away in the flip. Then, still standing in Corvin's own hall, she turned the same authority on Sorrel — the Oathkeeper's word and the banner's own weight together worth two favors paid out — and struck her from the citizenry outright. Sorrel Wick left that hall an Exile, purse considerably fuller than the office had ever paid her, and answering to no seat but her own from here on.

Sorrel didn't go far. Three days to clear out of Buried Giant, and she spent the first of them taking it — seven warbands against a Bandit token nobody had bothered replacing with real troops, five planted on the ground once the smoke cleared. A second visit to Riots, still sitting untouched at the table, turned one more secret into three favor. Then, with the purse still heavy, she bought the People's Favor back outright, the same banner Lilianne had just exiled her for holding — hers again by nightfall, on ground that answers to no one but her. What Supply remained went out into the wider world; a piper for hire turned up but Perrin's own ground bars his kind from anyone's table but his, so she came home with the same three advisers she left with.

## Round 7

The banner she'd bought back didn't stay bought — Corvin took it from her, then Lilianne took it from him, and it settled in the Chancellor's own hands well past anything Sorrel could match. But between messengers, a fever took her, a song of secrets nobody had guarded — she rose holding the last Vision the world deck had to give: Faith, and the Darkest Secret to answer it. Untouched all game, waiting for nobody. She took it outright and declared her hand to the whole table. Come her own next waking, unless something else moves first, Sorrel Wick wins.

## Round 8

Something else moved first. Word reached Lilianne in time, and she didn't come to win a fight — nine dice, Verdflayme's own fire, and every warband burned dead after the roll was already decided, the same ground Sorrel had taken with her own hands three days into exile. No hesitation at the door this time, no half-finished sentence — just the banishment, total, the way you'd settle a debt. The win that was one waking away is gone, and so, it seemed, was whatever was left of the friend under the Chancellor's mantle. Sorrel spent what turned out to be her last day of the game quietly — two more warbands called to her side, three advisers still at her table, no ground left to her name and no bitterness spent chasing what wasn't there to take.

Corvin sent Sorrel word that he'd noticed what her own day had cost her — then, with the round running short, took the very same banner back from her the same afternoon, trading on Hesper's own good name at Tinker's Fair to raise the coin for it. He didn't linger to see whether Lilianne wanted anything else out of Charming Valley either, and rode for the Hinterland with the banner in hand and an empty board behind him — only to find Narrow Pass itself barring the way any further than the pass.

Lilianne herself rode a python three regions to meet Perrin at the Ruined Temple, offering him back everything a Cray had lost there — the valley restored, a seat at her own table, even the Sorcerer's own mask that had broken his grandfather, held out this time as a gift instead of a wound. He turned all of it away on the ground it was offered on. She named him her enemy before she left, plainly, and didn't wait for dawn to finish breaking.

Enemy now by her own word, Perrin marched five companies on Narrow Pass a third time, this time meaning to finish it. The line held harder than bandit steel alone ever should have, and it cost him two good men to break it — but break it did. Every stretch of the Hinterland answers to Perrin Ashe now.

## Round 7

Lilianne worked her own Cradle like a woman closing accounts — a secret into three favor at Forest Council, fresh hands mustered at Magician's Code and set down at Charming Valley's own gate, before turning for home. Forest Paths carried her to Deep Woods for the price of a favor rather than a single supply, and the Old Oak took another secret off her hands for three favor more, same as it had the very first day she ever stood before it. From her own hall she reached clear across the Cradle to seat a Special Envoy at Charming Valley's table, the Chancellor's own privilege stretching further than her feet had to. Then, with favor enough finally banked, she paid five for the People's Favor a second time — out of Corvin's hands now, back where an Oathkeeper's word still counted for something.

Sorrel searched the last of the world deck and found the game's final Vision waiting for her: Faith, answered by the Darkest Secret, untouched since the day the game began. She took it outright and declared her hand to the whole table — come her own next waking, unless something else moves first, Sorrel Wick wins.

Corvin, banner-less again and hearing what Sorrel had found, traded the last secret he'd carried since the Mine at the pass itself, then walked on into Perrin's own valley to spend the coin calling two fresh hands to his side, and sent Colm Rooke ahead with a warning for Perrin — that whatever else stood between them, only Perrin's own turn stood between Sorrel and the whole game now.

With the Chancellor's own day looking all but decided, Corvin traded away his last secret at a bubbling lake, called two fresh hands to his side at the old mine beyond it, and sent Colm Rooke to Perrin one final time — not a warning this time, but a plain question about an old key, in case there was no later day left to ask it.

Perrin sent an enemy a warning that cost him nothing to give and everything to withhold — word to Lilianne, plain and unasked-for, of what Sorrel now held and how little time was left to answer it. Then, rather than bank the day quiet, he pulled two companies off Narrow Pass's own line and marched them into Wastes, ground he'd never once set foot on. Two swords found their mark, one man paid for it, and the bandits' own green fire went out without laying a hand on him. He planted his last man on the empty ground and made camp holding more of the map than anyone else at the table — four sites now answer to Perrin Ashe, one more than the Empire itself.

## Round 8

Perrin's warning reached Lilianne in time. She moved a favor off her own banner at first light, traded one more secret with the Old Oak that had never once turned her away, then took a single warband from Deep Woods and rode hard for Buried Giant — no stops, no second stall. There she called every hand Gossip and Riots could muster, seven strong, and fell on Sorrel Wick with the whole of it: the site, the pawn, and the Darkest Secret all named in the same breath, Naiad's own Rangers spending a favor to sharpen the attack further. Eight swords answered one shield. Sorrel's Vision of Faith went out with the banner it depended on — the last secret guarding it burned, one left, now Lilianne's to hold. She banished Sorrel to Narrow Pass and, for good measure, let Sticky Fire finish what the dice had already decided, and not one of Sorrel's warbands walked away from Buried Giant. Four companies stayed to hold the ground; three came home with her to Deep Woods' own ledger. The game goes on, one Wake later than it nearly ended.

## The End

War Exhaustion closed the book before anyone could write another line into it. When the table finally checked who the Empire itself answered to, there was no contest left in it — five relics carried since a green flame first left the Old Oak's own grove, and both banners besides, the People's Favor and the Darkest Secret alike, sitting in the Chancellor's own strongbox. Lilianne of the Woods, eighteen years old when the mantel first fell too large on her shoulders, stood confirmed Oathkeeper of the Cradle. Her father's throne, held.

## Epilogue

The Empire kept what it held and let the rest go — Fertile Valley, the Mine, Narrow Pass, and Wastes passed under truce to a new name, Ashvale, Perrin Ashe its Warden, Corvin Varn standing beside him more often than not. Two months into the peace, Sorrel Wick came through on her own two feet, no banner and no crown left to her name, and asked to meet them both at the Ruined Temple — the one corner of the new country nobody had claimed yet. She left it with an offer of ground to pick when she was ready, a night to think it over, and an old debt about a woman named Isolde finally answered plain instead of left folded shut. She took the mill's spare room rather than the road back out.

A month later, Corvin rode south for the first time since the truce, back across the Narrow Pass to stand on the ground his grandmother died on. He found Drowned City quieter than any painting had ever shown it, and found the Chancellor herself there on a private errand of her own besides. Over tea neither of them had poured, Lilianne gave him the piece of the story no one else living still held: Isolde had let Wren Sable take the blame for a crime that was hers, and only confessed it the day she died. Neither of them left the meeting quite the way they'd arrived — Corvin with a heavier debt and a lighter grudge, Lilianne with a name to go looking for once her own business at the water's edge was done.
